Famous vineyards

Catena Zapata

official page

Bodega Catena Zapata is a family-owned winery located in Mendoza, in the sub appellation of Agrelo, Argentina. The winery structure is distinguished by its pyramid-like design based on Mayan architecture.
The winery was founded in 1902 by Italian immigrant Nicola Catena and was passed to his son Domingo. Domingo’s son, Nicolás Catena Zapata (A.K.A. Nicolás Catena), was a pioneer to introduce European winemaking techniques to Argentina, including the introduction of Malbec and vine growing in high altitudes. Nicolás Catena’s daughter, Laura Catena, and other members of the Zapata family, have also been involved in the winery.

Rutini

official page

Felipe Rutini (born December 3, 1866 in Torre San Patrizio , Province of Ascoli Piceno , " Le Marche " Region , Italy ). He is known for founding Rutini Wines , Bodega La Rural and the Mendoza Wine Museum . He was one of the 4 great Italian winemakers in Mendoza.
The winery, which had been born with only two production blocks and the family home, set up new and extensive facilities in 1910 to complete the integral process of creating the first Argentine fine wines. The advanced technology machinery was imported from Europe. As a growth strategy, Felipe Rutini opened new and prosperous domestic markets, mainly in Buenos Aires and Santa Fe.

Luigi Bosca

official page

Bodega Luigi Bosca was created in 1901 by the Arizu Family in the Province of Mendoza. Its founders have managed to continue the tradition of making excellent wines, transmitting their wisdom for four generations and positioning themselves as benchmarks for Argentine wines in the world. Luigi Bosca is recognized for the quality of its wines, its deep knowledge of the terroir and the constant search for excellence through innovation and technology.
